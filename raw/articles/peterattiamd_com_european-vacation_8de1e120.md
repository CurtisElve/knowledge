Yes, there are the obvious differences—in Europe the portions seem smaller, they seem to contain

vacation there, but I still find that ‘on vacation’ in North America we still eat quicker).
Europe also lends itself to more walking and coffee and myriad other things that make what I’m about

All of these caveats aside, I am becoming convinced there is something different about the
starches—especially the breads and pastas—there versus here.
I don’t yet have enough information to suggest I know what I’m talking about, so this is pure
hypothesis generation: Is it different strains of wheat?
Is it different ingredients used alongside the wheat?
But here is what I observed on my recent trip to Italy.
I went out of my way to never say “no” to food, which meant eating more pasta and bread in one week
