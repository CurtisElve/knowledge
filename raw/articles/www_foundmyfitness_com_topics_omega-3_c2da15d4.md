Omega-3 fatty acids play critical roles in human health, participating in a wide range of
biochemical pathways, modulating the expression of hundreds of genes, and affecting multiple organ
systems throughout the lifespan, from _in utero_ development through old age.
These essential nutrients are available in both food and supplemental forms and vary considerably in
their bioavailability due to both characteristics of the forms as well as interindividual
differences in age, overall health, disease status, genetics, and other factors.
