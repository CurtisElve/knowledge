Consumption of salmon roe is growing in popularity.
For some people, however, an appreciation for any type of roe is an acquired one, due to the tiny
eggs' unique texture and briny flavor.
In particular, they burst with an audible “pop” between the tongue and the roof of the mouth,
releasing the lightly flavored contents.
Compared to the roe of other fishes, salmon roe has a considerably larger egg size, conspicuous pop,
and mild, sweet finish, faintly reminiscent of salmon.
Many roes, especially brined ones, readily absorb other flavors, especially those of certain metals.
